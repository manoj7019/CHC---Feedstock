CHC Must-Knows

CHCs democratize access to farm machinery, enabling small and marginal farmers to manage crop residue affordably, prevent stubble burning, and increase productivity.
Government-run CHCs reach more farmers across larger areas for in-situ services, while private CHCs excel in higher-margin ex-situ operations, often limiting rentals to their direct group.
Despite major investments, more than half of CRM machines are underutilized. Targeting underused private and farmer-run CHCs offers big potential for improving supply.
Packaged service bundles (machinery, tractor, operator) deliver far higher profits than machine-only rentals. These are preferred by farmers and provide reliable income to operators.
Digital booking platforms are rarely adopted. Most rentals happen through direct phone calls or face-to-face communication; user-friendly digitization is still needed.
Maintenance and labor costs are high, eating into margins. Stronger technical training, consistent maintenance, and digital tracking are required.
Key reforms: Link equipment subsidies to actual CHC performance, introduce asset lifecycle planning, improve financing, and train operators regularly.
Top strategy: Map inventories locally, prioritize partnerships with underutilized CHCs, and offer bundled or incentive-based contracts for maximum impact.
